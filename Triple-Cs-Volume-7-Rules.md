## 2. Triples Rules (UPRO, TQQQ, SPXL, UDOW)

- **Allocation**: 10% sweet spot in bull market; can range 10–30% depending on market conditions
- **Preferred instruments**: Only major-index triples — UPRO (S&P), TQQQ (Nasdaq), SPXL (S&P), UDOW (Dow). Sector-specific triples (SOXL, TECL, LABU) decay badly over time
- **Trim rule**: Trim every time triples collectively rise ~5% above target (e.g., $105K → trim $5K back to $100K)
- **Trim on up days**: Trim on every 1% up day in the markets
- **After rally**: Trim back to original core position of ~10%
- **Redeploy trimmed capital**: Into income-producing names (Cornerstone, Roundhill, Defiance, RexShares, Yieldmax)
- **Downturn buying**: Buy $100K in triples every 10% down from highs (up to $300K at 30% correction)
- **1/3 rule**: When selling income funds during downturns, buy 1/3 of the sold amount back in triples
- **Triples don't give qualifying income** — must rotate back to income names after rally from lows

## 3. Triple Shorts / Hedge Rules

- **Always hold inverse triple shorts**: Minimum 1% of portfolio at all times
- **Scale up with market**: Increase to 2–3% when market tops out; 5–10% during crashes
- **Hedge pairs**:
  - SPXU / SPXS ↔ SPXL / UPRO
  - SQQQ ↔ TQQQ
  - SDOW ↔ UDOW
  - SOXS ↔ SOXL
  - FNGD ↔ FNGU
- **Straddle at highs**: 1:1 long-to-short ratio
- **Strangle at 10%+ correction**: 2:1 long-to-short ratio
- **After 10% bounce from lows**: Trim longs AND rebalance shorts back to $1,500–$3,000 each
- **Every 10% up in SPY**: Rebalance triple shorts with proceeds from trimming longs
- **DIPS / CRSH**: Yieldmax inverse short income ETFs — DIPS for NVDY, CRSH for TSLY. Use to hedge Yieldmax positions.

---

## 4. Cornerstone Rules (CLM / CRF)

- **Core holding**: CLM and CRF are the "C's" — 4-star rated
- **DRIP at NAV**: Corporate DRIP buys shares at NAV (not market price) — this is the key mechanic
- **30%+ premium = sell/box signal**: When premium-to-NAV exceeds 30%, consider selling or boxing shares
- **Cashflow math**: 20% dividend × 5 years = 100% return on cost basis
- **At market highs**: Box Cornerstone shares to lock in gains
- **During downturns**: Increase Cornerstone in small amounts as market falls

## 5. Income Fund Family Nuances

### Defiance (QQQY, IWMY, WDTE, JEPY, BDTE, IDTE, QDTU)
- Sells **puts** to generate income
- Premiums increase in down markets
- Falls less than market on down days
- Index mappings: QQQY = Nasdaq, IWMY = Russell 2000, WDTE = S&P 500
- **Note**: Changing structure to calls — reduction in holdings may be prudent until behavior is confirmed
- Sell Defiance FIRST during downturns (to harvest tax losses and rotate into triples)

### Roundhill (XDTE, QDTE, RDTE, YBTC, WEEK)
- Sells **calls** to generate income
- Yields increase in up markets (calls appreciate as prices rise)
- Less volatile in down markets than RexShares/Yieldmax
- Index mappings: XDTE = S&P 500, QDTE = Nasdaq, RDTE = Russell 2000
- **Preferred for safety**: Switch from RexShares to Roundhill after bounces for safer positioning

### RexShares (AIPI, FEPI)
- Bounce back well from lows, then decay
- Eventually cap their upside
- Trim after bounce, bring back to original position size
- Then switch proceeds to safer Roundhill & Defiance

### Yieldmax (TSLY, NVDY, CONY, YMAX, YMAG, ULTY, etc.)
- Bounce back well from lows, then decay (similar to RexShares)
- Exorbitant yields, especially Mag 7 names
- Use YMAG DRIP strategy
- Hedge with inverse short income ETFs (DIPS for NVDY, CRSH for TSLY)
- Use FNGA/FNGB/FNGU with Yieldmax Mag 7 names for extra capital gains
- Hedge those with FNGD when needed
- DRIP automatically, but DRIP manually on dips when using margin or oversized positioning

- When buying 1/3 triples on the way down, margin leverage eventually disappears naturally
- DRIP manually (not auto) when using margin to control interest payments

- **Warning threshold**: 15% (approaching cap)
- **Fund family diversification**: Keep balance across all dividend fund families so no single family takes you down

## 8. AFW (Available For Withdrawal) Rules

- **AFW is the lifeblood of the account** — let it guide buy/sell decisions
- Position sizes and AFW dictate moves, not media
- **AFW drops 10% = market dropped 10%** → signal to BUY (not sell or do nothing)
- **Position size drops** (e.g., YMAG $10K → $9K) → rebalance $1K back into that position
- Same logic applies in reverse (position grows → trim back)
- **Rinse and repeat during every market correction, big or small**

---

## 9. Options Rules

- **Buy put options for insurance** (see Volume 5 for details)
- **0DTE puts**: Only in crashes — decay too much in normal down markets
- **Strikes**: 10% away from current price
- **Expirations**: 0–15 DTE
- **Sell puts on long triples at lows**: DCA/reload positions via premium collection
- **Sell puts on riskier names** (SOXL, TECL, DFEN) to lower risk exposure
- **At market highs**: Add back triple shorts, box Cornerstone, buy put options

## 10. Downturn Playbook (Step-by-Step)

1. Sell income funds first (Defiance and Roundhill first — they fall less, so you're selling at relative gains)
2. Buy 1/3 of sold amount in triples
3. Deleverage margin completely
4. Harvest tax losses from sold positions
5. Buy $100K in triples every 10% down from highs (up to $300K at 30% correction)
6. Increase triple shorts to 5–10% to straddle new long positions
7. Buy put options for insurance

### Recovery Playbook:
1. When market bounces 10% from lows: trim longs AND rebalance shorts back to $1,500–$3,000 each
2. Every 10% up in SPY: rebalance triple shorts using proceeds from trimming longs
3. After rally from lows: rotate triples back into income names (C's, Defiance, Roundhill, RexShares)
4. Trim RexShares/Yieldmax after bounce (they decay after initial recovery)
5. Switch proceeds to safer Roundhill & Defiance for broad index exposure
